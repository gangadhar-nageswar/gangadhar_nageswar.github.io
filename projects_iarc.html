International Aerial Robotics Competition (IARC) - 2021

Aerial Manipulation   Autonomous Navigation   Controls Subsystem   ROS   Gazebo

The AUVSI (Association for Unmanned Vehicle Systems International Foundation) International Aerial Robotics Competition is the longest-running collegiate aerial robotics challenge globally. The primary purpose of the IARC is to move state-of-the-art aerial robotics forward through the creation of significant and useful mission challenges that are considered ‘impossible’ at the time that they are proposed.

Due to the CoVID-19 situation, the competition was changed to online mode, where we had to develop the software for autonomous navigation, object detection, manipulation and also design the drone and manipulator. The problem statement can be found here. The main task can be summarised as autonomously navigating to a mast on which a communication module is present, which should be replaced by the one carried by drone within a time frame of 9 mins.

Please watch our submission to get a detailed overview of our design and the entire mission.

The competition required us to achieve the following abilities:

• Precision manipulation of large objects

• Fast outdoor operations over long distance

• Interaction with moving frames of reference

• Aerial robotic repair of mobile platforms

• Optical recognition

• Using ONLY onboard computing

I worked on designing the communications module gripper and providing optimal control to it, to precisely manipulate an object which is undergoing a sea motion with sea-states upto level 3 (highly vigorous motion). This was done using ROS framework and the entire mission was simulated in Gazebo.

Gripper Mechanism

The replacement mechanism is attached to the daughter drone and is the most integral component of the design. It consists of two grippers- one meant for holding on to the mast and the other for removing the module- each with two degrees of freedom. The replacement mechanism is made with light-weight but sturdy material to keep the mass to a minimum and thus reducing the load on the daughter drone.

Maneuvers for module replacement:

1. The first step comprises lowering the gripper and grabbing the swaying mast, thus ceasing any relative motion between the gripping mechanism and the mast.

2. Next, the communication module is extracted from the mast by employing a specially designed gripper which will slide onto the module’s antenna and pull it out using a linear actuator. As soon as the module loses contact with the guiding rods, it will fall automatically.

3. Now the replacement module can be slid into place, completing the process of module replacement.
