Self-driving Car (SeDriCa) UMIC, IIT Bombay

Autonomous Systems   Decision Making   Controls Subsystem   ROS   NMPC

We are a team of 20+ students aiming to develop India's first self-driving car, trying to achieve Level 5 Autonomy, capable of autonomous driving under Indian Road Conditions. Our team consists mainly of five subsystems: controls, path planning, decision making, localisation and computer vision. Currently we are aiming at Intelligent Ground Vehicle Challenge(IGVC) 2022, particularly the Self Drive Challenge held at Oakland University, Michigan.

I worked on decision making module which integrated with path planning provides the ego-vehicle optimal path in various scenarios, which includes lane changing, lane merging and encounter with traffic signs. We implemented a finite-state machine for the different traffic signs which can be encountered.

I also worked in controls subsystem, where we implemented non-linear MPC to provide optimal control to the vehicle to track the path planned. The current position of the vehicle, the path to be tracked and vehicle dynamics are model along with other mechanical constraints are fed into the NMPC algorithm, which solves an the optimisation problem and returns the vehicle steer angle and acceleration.

Besides from the above the two subsystems, I gained knowledge how all the subsystems interact with each other, essentially giving me a good insights into the pipeline of how autonomous systems operate.
